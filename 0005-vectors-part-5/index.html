Vector Operations
Cross Product
Vector Cross Product
  • Multiplying a vector by a vector
  • Also known as outer product
  • 3D vectors only
  • Yields a 3D vector and is not commutative
Cross Product
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x_1 \\ y_1 \\ z_1 \end{smallmatrix} \right] \times \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x_2 \\ y_2 \\ z_2 \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} y_1z_2 - z_1y_2 \\ z_1x_2 - x_1z_2 \\ x_1y_2 - y_1x_2 \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
Cross Product Example
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 4 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{smallmatrix} \right] \times \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} -3 \\ 7 \\ 9 \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} (1)(9) - (2)(7) \\ (2)(-3)-(4)(9) \\ (4)(7)-(1)(-3) \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} -5 \\ -42 \\ 31 \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
Cross Product
Yields a perpendicular vector to the original two vectors
Cross Product
Its magnitude is equal to product of magnitudes of original vectors and the sine of the angle between them
$\left\Vert a \times b \right\Vert = \left\Vert a \right\Vert \left\Vert b \right\Vert sin \theta$
Cross Product

If either $a$ or $b$ are parallel, or is the zero vector then $a \times b = 0$
The zero vector is parallel to every other vector (remember the zero vector is perpendicular for the dot product)

Cross Product

Its direction depends on left-handed or right-handed coordinate system
We determine by placing tail of $b$ to head of $a$ and determine whether we turn clockwise or counterclockwise from $a$ to $b$.
In left-hand, $a \times b$ points toward you if clockwise, and away if counterclockwise.
In right-hand, it's the opposite.

Cross Product
  • Anticommutative: $a \times b = -(b \times a)$
  • Not associative
  • $a \times a = 0$
  • $a \cdot (a \times b) = 0$ (because cross product produces perpendicular vector and dot product of perpendicular is zero)
Cross Product
Uses: creating a vector perpendicular to a plane, triangle, or polygon.
