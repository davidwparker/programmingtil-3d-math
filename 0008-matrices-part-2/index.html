Transposition
The transpose of a ($r\times c$) matrix $M$, denoted as $M^T$, is the $c\times r$ matrix where each column is made up of the rows of M.
$M^T_{ij} = M_{ji}$.
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} m_{11} & m_{12} & m_{13} \\ m_{21} & m_{22} & m_{23} \end{smallmatrix} \right]^T = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} m_{11} & m_{21} \\ m_{12} & m_{22} \\ m_{13} & m_{23} \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 3 & 4 & 2 \\ 7 & 9 & 1 \\ 5 & 6 & 8 \end{smallmatrix} \right]^T = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 3 & 7 & 5 \\ 4 & 9 & 6 \\ 2 & 1 & 8 \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
Vector Transposition
Row vectors become column vectors and vice versa.
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 3 & 4 & 2 \end{smallmatrix} \right]^T = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
Transposition notes:
  • $(M^T)^T = M$
  • $D^T = D$
