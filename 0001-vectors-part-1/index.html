Vectors Part 1
What are vectors
A vector is a list (or array) of numbers.
Dimension of a vector is how many numbers it contains.
We mostly care about 2D, 3D, and 4D vectors.
Notation
Horizontal (row vector): $[0,1,2]$
Vertical (column vector): $\left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
2d: $x, y$
3d: $x, y, z$
4d: $x, y, z, w$
Examples
2d: $x, y$
$a = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
$a_1 = a_x = 0$
$a_2 = a_y = 1$
Examples
3d: $x, y, z$
$a = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
$a_1 = a_x = 0$
$a_2 = a_y = 1$
$a_3 = a_z = 2$
Examples
4d: $x, y, z, w$
$a = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
$a_1 = a_x = 0$
$a_2 = a_y = 1$
$a_3 = a_z = 2$
$a_4 = a_w = 3$
A vector is just a directed line segment with magnitude and direction
Magnitude is the length of the vector
Direction is the way the vector is pointing in space
Illustration
Displacement: "walk backwards two steps"
Velocity: "the train is going South at 20MPH"
Displacement and velocity are different than distance and speed. The former two are vector quantities and have a direction. The latter two are scalar quantities and do not have a direction.
Vectors are numbered with a signed displacement.
In 2D: $[1,2]$ means to $x$ is forward $1$ and $y$ is up $2$
Vectors can be represented by a sequence of displacements.
In the previous 2D example: the sequence $[1,0]$, $[0,2]$ would result in the same $[1,2]$
A vector is different than a point. A point specifies location, whereas a vector specifies magnitude and direction.
The point $(2,1)$ can be reached given the vector $[2,1]$, relative to the origin of the coordinate system.
