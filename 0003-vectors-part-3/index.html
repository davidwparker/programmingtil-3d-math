Distance Formula
The Distance Formula
$d = b-a = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} b_x-a_x \\ b_y-a_y \\ b_z-a_z \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
$distance(a,b) = \left\Vert d\right\Vert = \sqrt{d_x^2 +d_y^2+d_z^2}$

Substituing d:
$distance(a,b) = \left\Vert b-a\right\Vert = $

$\sqrt{(b-a)_x^2 +(b-a)_y^2+(b-a)_z^2}$
Example
$distance([2\:3],[8\:-5]) = \left\Vert b-a\right\Vert$

$= \sqrt{(8-2)^2 + (-5-3)^2}$
$= \sqrt{6^2 + 8^2}$
$= \sqrt{36 + 64}$
$= \sqrt{100}$
$= 10$
