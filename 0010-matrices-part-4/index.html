How does a Matrix Transform Vectors
Recall:
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{smallmatrix} \right] + \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 0 \\ y \\ 0 \end{smallmatrix} \right] + \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ z \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
Rewritten:
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{smallmatrix} \right] = x \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{smallmatrix} \right] + y \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{smallmatrix} \right] + z \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
Rewrite unit vectors p, q, r:
$v = xp + yq + zr$
p, q, and r are known as base vectors.
Using p, q, r to make matrix M:
$ M = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} p \\ q \\ r \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} p_x & p_y & p_z \\ q_x & q_y & q_z \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
Multiplying a vector by this, we get
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x & y & z \end{smallmatrix} \right] \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} p_x & p_y & p_z \\ q_x & q_y & q_z \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
$ = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} xp_x + yq_x + zr_x & xp_y + yq_y + zr_y & xp_z + yq_z + zr_z \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
$ = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} xp & yq & zr \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
What does this mean?
If we interpret the rows of a matrix as the basis vectors of a coordinate system,
then multiplication by the matirx performs a coordinate system transformation.
if $aM=b$ then we say that $M$ transformed $a$ to $b$.
Multiplication = Transformation
Matrices are basically a compact way to represent math operations needed to perform
a coordinate space transformation.
