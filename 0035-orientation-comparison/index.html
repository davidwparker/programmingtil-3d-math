Comparison of Orientation Representations Matrices, Euler Angles, Quaternions
Rotating points between coodinate spaces
Matrix: Possible
Euler: Impossible (convert to Matrix)
Quat: Impossible (convert to Matrix)
Concatenation or incremental rotation
Matrix: Possible. Usually slower than Quat. Matrix creep
Euler: Impossible
Quat: Possible. Usually faster than matrix.
Interpolation
Matrix: Impossible
Euler: Possible. Aliasing may cause Gimbal locks.
Quat: Possible. Slerp provides smooth interpolations.
Human Friendly
Matrix: No
Euler: Yes
Quat: No
Storage size
Matrix: 9 numbers
Euler: 3 numbers
Quat: 4 numbers
Representation is unique for given orientation
Matrix: Yes
Euler: No
Quat: Two distinct representations for any orientation, which are negates of one another.
Can become invalid?
Matrix: creep can occur
Euler: Any three numbers are valid
Quat: Error creep can occur
Notes:
Euler is great for humans to work with. Don't forget that. Optimize later, when needed
Matrix must be used if vector coordinate space transformations are needed.
For animations, use something smaller (Euler or Quat)
Quats are great at interpolation
