Combining Transformations
In gaming, or 3d rendering, suppose we want to show an object
with its own position and orientation in the world,
and we want to render it given a campera in its own position and orientation.
We will transform the object vertices and transform from object to world space
and World space to Camera Space.
$ P_{world} = P_{object}M_{object\rightarrow world}$
$ P_{camera} = P_{world}M_{world\rightarrow camera}$
$ P_{camera} = (P_{object}M_{object\rightarrow world})M_{world\rightarrow camera}$
Recall that multiplication is associative
$ P_{camera} = (P_{object}M_{object\rightarrow world})M_{world\rightarrow camera}$
$ P_{camera} = P_{object}(M_{object\rightarrow world}M_{world\rightarrow camera})$
Concatenate matrices:
$ M_{object\rightarrow camera} = M_{object\rightarrow world}M_{world\rightarrow camera}$ $ P_{camera} = P_{object}M_{object\rightarrow camera}$

Geometrically, what's happening in matrix concatenation?
let $a_1, a_2, a_3$ be the rows of $A$
$ A = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
$ AB = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{smallmatrix} \right] B = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1B \\ a_2B \\ a_3B \end{smallmatrix} \right] $
