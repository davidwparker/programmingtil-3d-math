Vectors Part 2
Vector Operations
Negation, Magnitude, Normals, Addition, Subtraction
Zero Vector
0 = $\left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ 0 \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
Special as it's the only vector with a magnitude of zero and direction of zero.
Negation
$additive$ $inverse$ of $x$ -> ($-x$) is the element that yields the zero when added to $x$.
$x + (-x) = 0$
Negating a vector
$-\left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ a_n \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} -a_1 \\ -a_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ -a_n \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
Negating 2d, 3d, 4d
$-[x\:y] = [-x\:-y]$
$-[x\:y\:z] = [-x\:-y\:-z]$
$-[x\:y\:z\:w] = [-x\:-y\:-z\:-w]$
Vector Magnitude
Magnitude is also known as the length or norm.
In Linear Algebra
$\left\Vert v\right\Vert = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^n {v_i}^2}$
Examples
2D, 3D
$\left\Vert v\right\Vert = \sqrt{{v_x}^2+{v_y}^2}$
$\left\Vert v\right\Vert = \sqrt{{v_x}^2+{v_y}^2+{v_z}^2}$
Examples
3D: $\left\Vert [2 \:-3 \:4]\right\Vert$
$\sqrt{2^2+(-3)^2+4^2}$
$\sqrt{4+9+16}$
$\sqrt{29}$
Vector Multiplication by a Scalar
$k\left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ a_n \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ a_n \end{smallmatrix} \right] k = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} ka_1 \\ ka_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ ka_n \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
3D Example
$k\left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{smallmatrix} \right] k = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} kx \\ ky \\ kz \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
3D Example
Can also be divided by a nonzero scalar $\dfrac{v}{k} = \dfrac{1}{k}\left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} x/k \\ y/k \\ z/k \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
Multiplication Notes
  • No multiplication symbol
  • Scalar multiplied by vector (and division) happen before addition or subtraction
  • Scalar cannot be divided by a vector; vector cannot be divided by a vector
Normalized Vector

Often, we care about direction but not the magnitude.
So we use unit vectors.
A unit vector is a vector with a magnitude of 1.
A unit vector is also known as normalized vectors or normals.

Normalized Vector
$v_{norm} = \dfrac{v}{\left\Vert v\right\Vert}, v \neq 0$
Vector Addition
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ a_n \end{smallmatrix} \right] + \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} b_1 \\ b_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ b_n \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1+b_1 \\ a_2+b_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ a_n+b_n \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
Vector Subtraction
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ a_n \end{smallmatrix} \right] - \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} b_1 \\ b_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ b_n \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} a_1-b_1 \\ a_2-b_2 \\ . \\ . \\ . \\ a_n-b_n \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
Example
$ \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 1 \\ 5 \\ 4 \end{smallmatrix} \right] - \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} 1-4 \\ 5-3 \\ 4-0 \end{smallmatrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{smallmatrix} -3 \\ 2 \\ 4 \end{smallmatrix} \right]$
Addition/Subtraction Notes
  • A vector cannot be added/subtracted with a scalar or vector of a different dimension
  • Vector addition is commutative, but vector subtraction is not. $a+b = b+a$, but $a-b = -(b-a)$... $a-b=b-a$ only $a=b$
